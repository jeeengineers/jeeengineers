Keep Production Flowing: Proactive Maintenance & Quick Fixes

Unexpected machine breakdowns can halt production. However, many common issues can be quickly addressed with basic troubleshooting, while others can be prevented through regular maintenance.

Understanding warning signs, potential causes, and having a clear action plan allows operators to minimize downtime, ensuring reliable operation, consistent quality, and maximum output.

Common Issues and Troubleshooting Steps

Downtime is costly. Learn to quickly identify and resolve common problems with your brick making machine to maintain efficiency.

Symptoms: Bricks vary significantly in dimensions, feel light (low density), crumble easily, or fail compression tests.

Potential Causes:
  • Incorrect Raw Material Mix (improper ratio of fly ash, sand, lime, cement, water).
  • Inconsistent Material Feeding (uneven flow into mold box).
  • Insufficient Compaction Pressure (hydraulic system issues).
  • Worn Out Molds (damaged or worn, causing size variations).
  • Inadequate Curing (improper time, temperature, humidity).
Troubleshooting Steps:
  • Verify Mix Ratio: Double-check formulation, ensure accurate batching, test consistency.
  • Check Feeding System: Inspect conveyors/hoppers for blockages/speed issues. Ensure even flow.
  • Monitor Hydraulic Pressure: Check gauge against specs. Inspect for leaks/pump issues.
  • Inspect Molds: Examine for wear/damage. Replace if needed.
  • Review Curing Process: Ensure correct duration, moisture, and temperature.

Symptoms: Machine stops abruptly, straining sounds, mold box or ejector gets stuck.

Potential Causes:
  • Foreign Objects (stones, metal, hardened lumps in mix).
  • Overfilling the Mold Box.
  • Incorrect Mix Consistency (too dry or too wet).
  • Mechanical Obstruction (misaligned parts, loose bolts, damage).
  • Insufficient Lubrication (friction and binding).
Troubleshooting Steps:
  • Safety First: Stop machine, use lockout/tagout procedures.
  • Clear Obstructions: Inspect mold box, hopper, ejection area. Remove foreign objects/jammed material.
  • Adjust Material Feed: Reduce feed amount if overfilling.
  • Check Mix Consistency: Adjust water content.
  • Inspect Mechanical Parts: Check alignment, fasteners, damage. Repair/replace.
  • Lubricate: Follow manufacturer's schedule.

Symptoms: Slow operation, low pressure, visible oil leaks, overheating oil.

Potential Causes:
  • Low Hydraulic Fluid Level.
  • Clogged Hydraulic Filter.
  • Worn Hydraulic Pump.
  • Leaking Hoses or Fittings.
  • Incorrect Pressure Settings (relief valve).
  • Air in the System.
  • Overheating (insufficient cooling).
Troubleshooting Steps:
  • Check Fluid Level: Inspect and top up with recommended fluid.
  • Inspect/Replace Filter: Check condition, replace if clogged or per schedule.
  • Check Pump: Listen for noise, monitor pressure. Repair/replace if worn.
  • Inspect for Leaks: Examine hoses, fittings, seals, cylinders. Tighten/replace.
  • Verify Pressure Settings: Check relief valve setting. Adjust if needed (consult manual).
  • Bleed Air: Follow manufacturer's procedure.
  • Check Cooling System: Ensure cooler is clean and functional. Check oil temperature.

Symptoms: Fails to power on, unresponsive panel, error codes, motors not running, sensor issues.

Potential Causes:
  • Power Supply Problem (no power, tripped breaker, fuse).
  • Loose Wiring or Connections.
  • Faulty Switches or Buttons (E-stop engaged).
  • Sensor Malfunction (dirty, damaged, misaligned).
  • Motor Issues (overload tripped, faulty winding/starter).
  • PLC/Control Panel Fault (requires expert diagnosis).
Troubleshooting Steps:
  • Safety First: Disconnect power (lockout/tagout).
  • Check Power Supply: Verify main switch, breakers, fuses. Check voltage.
  • Inspect Wiring: Look for damage, loose connections, burnt smell. Tighten terminals.
  • Check Switches: Disengage E-stop. Test start/stop buttons.
  • Inspect Sensors: Clean, check alignment/connection. Test operation.
  • Check Motor Overloads: Reset if tripped. Investigate cause.
  • Consult Manual/Expert: Refer to diagrams. Contact manufacturer or electrician if needed.

Symptoms: Grinding, banging, squealing sounds, or excessive vibration.

Potential Causes:
  • Loose Components (bolts, nuts, parts).
  • Worn Bearings (motors, conveyors, moving parts).
  • Misalignment (conveyor belts, shafts).
  • Lack of Lubrication.
  • Foreign Object Interference.
  • Hydraulic System Issues (air, pump cavitation).
Troubleshooting Steps:
  • Identify Source: Pinpoint location of noise/vibration (use caution).
  • Stop and Inspect: Turn off (lockout/tagout). Check/tighten loose bolts/nuts.
  • Check Bearings: Listen near bearings, check for heat/play. Replace if worn.
  • Check Alignment: Inspect belts, chains, shafts. Adjust.
  • Lubricate: Follow schedule meticulously.
  • Inspect for Foreign Objects: Check pathways of moving parts.
  • Check Hydraulic System: Listen to pump, check for air.

Preventative Maintenance: The Best Troubleshooting

Regular maintenance is key to preventing many common issues:

Daily Checks

Visual inspection for leaks, loose parts, debris. Check fluid levels.

Lubrication

Follow manufacturer's schedule for greasing and oiling.

Cleaning

Keep machine, molds, and area clean. Remove buildup.

Filter Replacement

Change hydraulic and air filters as recommended.

Mold Care

Regularly inspect molds for wear/damage. Clean properly.

Tighten Fasteners

Periodically check and tighten bolts and nuts.

Follow manufacturer's recommendations for detailed scheduled inspections.

Conclusion

Timely troubleshooting and consistent preventative maintenance are crucial for optimal performance and longevity of brick making machines.
